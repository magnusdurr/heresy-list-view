Rules

EA - Heresy is based on the NetEA rules, which can be found over at the NetEA Tournament Pack site. However, we have also made a number of changes and additions to these rules, which can be found below under 'New Rules' headings. These new rules effectively makes EA - Heresy a different game from the core rules, and not particularly compatible with playing games against the 40k armies of e.g. NetEA, Epic UK, or EA:Ragnar√∂k. For more information about this, see the 'About' page.

In addition, there are a number of rules in the NetEA Epic Game Rules that can be interpreted in more than one way. Below the 'New Rules' are 'Rule interpretations & house-rules', that we have used when developing the EA - Heresy lists. We suggest you also use these when playing EA - Heresy games.

New Rules - Strategy Phase

The Strategy Rating

The Strategy rating is now between 3 to 1 (4 (Alpha Legion), 3 (Legiones Astartes), 2 (Solar Auxilia, 1 for Ruinstorm Cultists and Imperialis Militia). This number means the number of dice rolled when rolling to determine who goes first in a specific phase. In addition, the highest dice rolled will be modified by factors such as the number of allies, and who went first last turn, as seen in the table below.

Modifiers for Strategy Rolls

  • Army is contains no allies: +1
  • Army lost the strategy roll last turn: +1
  • Army has one allied faction: no modifier!
  • Army has two allied factions: -1
  • Army contains three allied factions: -2

In case of ties, the army with the higher strategy rating always wins. The strategy modifiers are set at the start of battle, and does not change during the game even if all allied formations are destroyed.

New Rules - Actions

Overwatch

Formations that successfully activates with an Overwatch order may also move 5 cm. This extra movement is voluntary (e.g. if a player wishes to avoid triggering enemy Overwatch).

Offboard Support

Calling for Offboard Support is a new action that a player can take after having completed an Advance, Overwatch, Marshal, or Sustain Fire action with an unbroken formation that contains a unit with the Vox-Caster special rule. This counts as retaining the initiative, and thus cannot be done if the initiative has already been retained once. The Offboard Support action automatically succeeds.

Unless otherwise specified in the description of the Offboard Support asset, the following procedure is used. Place a marker within x cm (depending on the type of Offboard Support) within Line of Sight of the unit with the Vox-Caster special rule to indicate where the Offboard Support asset will come down. The Offboard Support is then carried out according to its special rules before the start of the next friendly action, or before the end phase, depending on which comes first.

Types of Offboard Support:
Teleport

A formation consisting completely of units with the 'Teleport' special rule must be placed in the reserves before deployment. They may only enter the battlefield through an Offboard Support action. Immediately place the formation with all units completely within 15 cm of the unit with the 'Vox-Caster' special rule, and roll a D6 for each, to test if any teleport mishaps occured. For each roll of 1, the teleporting formation suffers a BM.

Teleporting units placed within 15 cm of any enemy units with the 'Psyker' or 'Daemonic' special rules suffer Teleport Mishap for any 1 and 2 rolled, instead of just 1.

Unlike other Offboard Support actions, Teleporting grants immediately grants the controlling player the chance to retain the initiative twice, meaning that the formation that just arrived may attempt carry out an action.

Drop Pods

A formation consisting completely of units of INF (without the 'Bulky', 'Jump Packs' or 'Mounted' special rules), and/or LV & AV with the 'Walker' special rules may be placed in Drop Pods. These must be placed in the reserves before deployment. They may only enter the battlefield through an Offboard Support action. Place the Drop Pod with its center within 15 cm of the unit with the 'Vox-Caster' special rule. Calling down a Drop Pod counts as an additional action, so the player using it may not retain the initiative afterwards. Once the Drop Pod arrives at the battlefield, move the Drop Pod marker up to 15 cm in any direction that does not place it in an enemy zone of control. Any enemy units within 10 cm of the centre of the drop pod are attacked by an AP6+/AT6+ (Disrupt) attack. After these attacks have been resolved, place the Drop Pods contents fully within 10 cm of the centre of the Drop Pod.

Dreadclaw

A formation consisting completely of units of INF (without the 'Jump Packs' or 'Mounted' special rules), and/or LV & AV with the 'Walker' special rules may be placed in Dreadclaws. These must be placed in the reserves before deployment. They may only enter the battlefield through an Offboard Support action. Place the Dreadclaw with its center within 15 cm of the unit with the 'Vox-Caster' special rule. Calling down a Dreadclaw counts as an additional action, so the player using it may not retain the initiative afterwards. Once the Drop Pod arrives at the battlefield, move the Dreadclaw marker up to 15 cm in any direction that does not place it in an enemy zone of control. Then place the Dreadclaw contents fully within 10 cm of the centre of the Dreadclaw.

Deathwind

A Deathwind attack places a marker within 15 cm of the model with 'Vox-Caster'. Once it arrives on the battlefield, it may be moved by 15 cm in any direction. Place the large barrage template with the centre hole over the marker. Any model under the template is attacked by a AP6+/AT6+ (Disrupt) attack.

Palisade Drop Pod

A Palisade Drop Pod may only be used alongside other Drop Pods, and is added to a formation in Drop Pods or Dreadclaws during deployment. All models in the formation counts as being in Cover and gains the Invulnerable Save special rule for the purposes of Overwatch when they exit their Drop Pods.

Orbital Pin-Point Attack

A Pin-Point attack has a range of 30 cm, and may only be targeted at War Engines. Once it arrives on the battlefield, it will inflict an automatic MW, TK(D3) attack on a single War Engine under the center of the marker.

Orbital Barrage

An Orbital Barrage has a range of 45 cm. Once it arrives on the battlefield, place the small barrage template with its centre over the marker. Any unit under the template is attacked by an AP4+/AT5+ (MW) attack.

Heavy Artillery Strike

A Heavy Barrage has a range of 60 cm. Once it arrives on the battlefield, place the large barrage template with its centre over the marker. Any unit under the template is attacked by an AP4+/AT6+ (Disrupt) attack.

Phospex Artillery Strike

A Phospex Strike has a range of 45 cm. Once it arrives on the battlefield, place the small barrage template with its centre over the marker. Any unit under the template is attacked by an AP4+/AT6+ (Disrupt, Ignore Cover) attack.

Orbital Rad Barrage

A Rad Barrage has a range of 45 cm. Once it arrives on the battlefield, place the large barrage template with its centre over the marker. Any unit under the template is attacked by an AP2+/AT6+ (Rad) attack.

Orbital Haywire Barrage

A Haywire Barrage has a range of 45 cm. Once it arrives on the battlefield, place the large barrage template with its centre over the marker. Any unit under the template is attacked by an AP6+/AT2+ (Rad) attack.

Minefield Submunitions Strike

A Minefield Submunitions attack has a range of 15 cm. Unlike other Offboard Support assets, there is no delay for this attack to arrive. Place a Minefield template (same as a small barrage template) with its centre over the marker. No part of the template may be placed over any terrain or model. For the rest of the battle, this area is now considered to be dangerous terrain.

Blind Artillery Strike

A Blind Burst has a range of 15 cm. Unlike other Offboard Support assets, there is no delay for this attack to arrive. Place the Blind template (same as a small barrage template) with its centre over the marker. No part of the template may be placed over any terrain (other than roads, water, or open ground) or on top of any model. For the rest of the battle, this area blocks line of sight and gives Cover just like terrain (it is in all other ways considered to be open ground, and does not give a cover save).

Deathstrike Attacks

Unlike other Offboard Support assets Deathstrike missiles have unlimited range, but are reliant on more precise targeting data. This means that any marker put down on the battlefield needs to be confirmed by a second Offboard Support action by a unit with 'Vox-Caster' before the attack will commence. All markers that have not been confirmed expire at the end of each turn.

Deathstrike Warp Missile Attack

A Vortex Missile attack may only be targeted at War Engines. Once it arrives on the battlefield, it will inflict an automatic MW, TK(D3), Shield Piercer attack on a single War Engine under the center of the marker.

Deathstrike Vortex Missile Attack

A Vortex Missile attack places a small barrage template over the marker. All units under it suffers an AP4+/AT4+, MW, TK(1), Shield Disruptor attack.

Deathstrike Plasma Missile Attack

A Plasma Missile attack places the large barrage template over the marker. All units under it suffers an AP4+/AT5+ attack.

Deathstrike Stasis Missile Attack

A Stasis Missile attack places the large barrage template over the marker. Any formation with a majority of units under the template suffers an additional -2 to activate this turn. Any formation with less than half of its units under the template suffers an -1 to activate this turn. In addition, any formation under it suffers a BM for coming under fire.

New Rules - Movement

Movement through friendly formations

INF may move freely through all types of units, and can be moved through freely.

Movement through enemy formations

Units may move past enemy formations when becoming broken or failing to rally while broken. This bonus movement ignores enemy zones of control, and may move over enemy units.

New Rules - Assaults

Countercharges

Units in formations that are Engaged may choose whether to counter-charge against the closest enemy unit in the formation that Engaged it, and may ignore already Engaged enemy models while doing so, or alternatively counter-charge against the closest enemy unit.

Units in formations that become Engaged do not have to stay in coherency while counter-charging, but must attempt to move back into coherency when the Assault is over.

Supporting Formations

Units do not ignore BMs when supporting an Assault, but are suppressed just like with Shooting (from back to front).

New Combat Resolution Modifier Table (Cumulative)

  • Each point of damage inflicted during the Assault: +1
  • More units than the opposing formation: +1
  • More than two times as many units as the opposing formation: +1
  • No Blast Markers: +1
  • Opponent has more Blast Markers: +1
  • Opponent has more Blast Markers than their combined number of units / DC, or is broken: +1
  • Each unit with the Inspiring special rule: +1

New Rules - War Engines

War Engine Basic Rules

  • WE no longer block LOS.
  • WE may allocate CC hits beyond base contact.
  • WE may not barge.
  • WE must allocate at least one dice to CC for each unit / DC in base contact during an assault.
  • WE may not use Extra Attacks in FF if engaged in CC.
  • WE CC attacks allocated towards INF suffer -1 to hit.
  • WE CC attacks allocated against other War Engines gain an additional +1 to hit.
  • Aircraft and BP type weapons gain an additional +1 to hit War Engines with less than 3 DC, and +2 to hit against WE with 3 or more DC.

Void Shields

Each Void Shield on an unbroken formation causes one BM when it goes down. Void Shields on broken formations do not cause BMs. Void Shields still does not count as kills during an Engage.

New Rules - Blast Markers, Breaking, Rallying

Formations only become broken after exceeding the number of units, not equaling them. This means that a formation can become completely suppressed without becoming broken.

Formations that attempt to rally suffer an additional -1 penalty if it is completely on the enemy half of the battlefield.

Only unbroken enemy units count for the ‚Äúenemies within 30 cm‚Äù check.

Formations that are completely inside a War Engine transport or Stronghold gains an additional +1 to rally.

New Rules - Aircraft

Hover

Aircraft with Hover are treated as AC with Bomber during their first approach to the battlefield. Once at the final location, they transition into becoming Support Craft (for WE) or Skimmers (for AV) for the rest of the battle.

Transitioning into becoming a Support Craft or Skimmer counts as movement for overwatch. An aircraft which transitions into a Support Craft may either shoot (with an additional -1 modifier to hit), or disembark any transported units. Disembarking from an Aircraft that is transitioning into a Support Craft does not count as an activation, but does count as movement for the purposes of Overwatch.

Support Craft

Support Craft may only contest objectives.

Support Craft Transports

Support Craft may pick up friendly formations on the battlefield by ending its movement close to or on top of the formation(s) that are to be picked up. If all units in that formation(s) are within 5 cm of the Support Craft, they may be picked up. Any units in the formation that cannot fit are destroyed, causing a BM on that formation for each destroyed unit.

Units with Jump Pack or Skimmer may disembark up to 15 cm away from Support Craft, instead of the usual 5 cm.

Support Craft may embark and disembark formations that have already activated this round, including broken formations.

Each time a Support Craft suffers a BM for coming under fire, each transported formation also suffers one BM. This includes any BMs suffered during the approach move.

Aircraft Movement

Aircraft no longer linger on the battlefield after carrying out ground attacks, interceptions, or CAP. Instead they immediately fly off after carrying out their action, and after any enemy formations have had a chance to carry out a CAP mission. Flying formations gain one BM for getting shot at while on the way in, and one on the way out as normal.

Aircraft CAP

Formations of Fighter and Fighter-Bomber AC may start the game on CAP. This counts as their action for the first turn.

Shooting at WE

Aircraft gain an additional +1 modifier when shooting at WE with 1-2 DC, and +2 when shooting at WE with 3 or more DC. This bonus does not apply to BP type weapons.

New Rules - Initiative Special Rules

Chivalry

All Questor Familia formations receive a +1 modifier to their Action Test when carrying out an Engage or Double action, and a +1 modifier to their Rally Tests.

Army of Mankind

All Solar Auxilia formations receive a +1 modifier to their Action Test when carrying out an Advance or Overwatch action, and gains a +1 modifier to their Rally Tests as long as the formation contains a unit with a Character.

Gloriam Mechanicus

All Mechanicum Taghmata formations receive a +1 modifier to their Action Test when carrying out a Sustain Fire or Marshal action, and a +1 modifier to their Rally Tests. Does not apply to formations that exclusively consists of units with the 'Machine Spirit' special rule.

Malefica Mechanica

All Dark Mechanicum formations receive a +1 modifier to their Action Test when carrying out an Advance, Double, or Engage action. Does not apply to formations that exclusively consists of units with the 'Machine Spirit' special rule.

Tide of Madness

All Ruinstorm formations receive a +1 modifier to their Action Test when carrying out an Engage action, and a +1 modifier to their Rally Tests as long as the formation contains a unit with the 'Daemonic' or 'Psyker - Anathemata' special rules.

The Muster of Worlds

All Imperialis Militia formations receive a +1 modifier to their Action Test when carrying out an Overwatch action, does not suffer a BM when forced to take a 'Hold' action, and gains a +1 modifier to their Rally Tests as long as the formation contains a unit with a Character.

New Rules - Unit Special Rules

Skirmisher

Units with the 'Skirmisher' special rule is in coherency if they are within 10 cm from another unit instead of the usual 5 cm. Units with 'Skirmisher' may garrison according to the same rules as units with 'Scout'.

Blitz

Units with the 'Blitz' special rule gains an additional 5 cm in speed when conducting an Engage action.

Evasive

Units with the 'Evasive' special rule gains an additional 5 cm in speed when consolidating after winning an assault. In addition, a unit with this special rule may consolidate after supporting a winning assault.

Tall

Units with the 'Tall' special rule counts as Support Craft for Line of Sight purposes, and do not benefit from cover when inside terrain.

Bulky

Units with the 'Bulky' special rule takes two spaces to transport.

Vox-Caster

A formation containing a unit with the 'Vox-Caster' special rule may be used for carrying out an Offboard Support action as detailed in the 'new actions' part of the rules.

Commander

A unit with the Commander special rule can order another unactivated formation to follow them when they make an assault, as long as the formation have at least one unit within 15 cm of the unit with the Commander special rule. Make a single initiative roll for both formations, counting a -1 modifier if any have Blast markers. If the test is failed then both formations receives a Blast marker. The formation with the Commander must take a hold action, but the other formation may take an action later in the turn. If the test is passed then both formations may take an engage action. Treat the two formations as if they were a single formation for all rules purposes for the duration of the assault. A 2D6 roll is used to resolve a combined assault. If the attackers lose then each formation is broken. If they win then each formation receives a number of Blast markers equal to the casualties it suffered in the combat. The Commander rule can also be used to defend against enemy assaults, by declaring that any friendly formation within 5 cm becomes intermingled in the assault, in the same manner as if the opponent had done this declaration. Defensive and Offensive use of the Commander special rule may only be done once for each side in an assault, no matter how many units with Commander are involved.

Dauntless

Units with Dauntless can take 2 Blast Markers before they are suppressed, or to kill a unit in a broken formation (ignore any leftover Blast Markers). Dauntless formations are only broken if they have more than 2 Blast Markers per unit in the formation. Dauntless formations only count half their number of Blaster markers in assault resolution (rounding down to a minimum of one). Halve the number of extra hits suffered by a Dauntless formation that loses an assault, rounding down in favour of the Dauntless formation. When a broken formation with Dauntless rallies, it receives a number of Blast Markers equal to the number of units, rather than half this number.

Vanguard

Formations that contains at least one unit with the 'Vanguard' special rule does not suffer -1 to rally when completely on the enemy half of the table.

Steadfast

When broken or otherwise forced to take a withdrawal move, a unit with the 'Steadfast' special rule will only be destroyed if it ends its move within 5cm of the enemy rather than 15 cm. In addition, any formation consisting completely of units with this special rule only suffers a -1 modifier to rally if within 15 cm of an unbroken enemy formation instead of the usual 30 cm.

Split Fire

A War Engine with this special rule may declare that it will Split Fire when shooting with any action. The WE may fire at up to two enemy formations with an additional -1 modifier to hit, but may not target more than one formation per individual weapon.

Daemonic

Units with the 'Daemonic' special rule counts as being twice as many (or having twice as many DC) for determining 'outnumber' during assault. Units with the 'Daemonic' special rule are automatically destroyed if it is part of a formation that loses an assault.

Machine Spirit

A formation that exclusively consists of units with the 'Machine Spirit' special rule may only perform the Hold, Engage, Marshal, Sustained Fire, and Overwatch actions. In addition, such formations may only contest objectives, and always counts as being within 30cm of an enemy formation when attempting to Rally.

Silica Animus

A unit with the 'Silica Animus' is an abominable and soulless artificial general intelligence awakened by forbidden rites pursued by the Dark Mechanicus...

Teleport Homer

Units that Teleport within 15 cm from a friendly, unbroken unit with Teleport Homer already on the battlefield may re-roll 1:s when testing for Teleport Mishap. May not be used if there are any unbroken enemy units with the 'Psyker' or 'Daemonic' special rules within 15 cm of the unit.

Transport (x)

Units with the 'Transport (x)' special rule may transport "x" number of INF, which may not have the 'Bulky' or 'Mounted' special rules.

Heavy Transport (x)

Units with the 'Heavy Transport (x)' special rule may transport "x" number of INF, which may have the 'Bulky' special rule. Each 'Bulky' unit takes two spaces each.

Assault Transport (x)

Units with the 'Assault Transport (x)' special rule may transport "x" number of INF, which may have the 'Bulky' and 'Mounted' special rules. In addition, it may treat AV or LV with the 'Walker' special rule as INF. Any unit with these special rules take two spaces to carry.

New Rules - Weapon Special Rules

Macro-Weapon

The MW special rule ignores armour as normal, but in EA - Heresy such weapons will always have their weapon profile split into AP/AT/AA parts.

Energetic

Weapons with the 'Energetic' special rule gains an additional +1 to hit when the unit's formation conducts a Sustain Fire action.

Titan Hunter

Weapons with the 'Titan Hunter' special rule gains an additional +1 to hit against War Engines.

Crippling

A weapon with the 'Crippling' special rule causes critical hits on War Engines on a 5+.

Rad

Weapons with the 'Rad' special rule causes BMs for each hit inflicted, just like weapons with the 'Disrupt' special rule. Unlike 'Disrupt', the 'Rad' special rule does not force their targets to roll armour saves, and they are not removed as casualties for failing to save.

√Üther-Fire

Weapons with the '√Üther-Fire' special rule gains an additional +1 to hit when the unit's formation conducts an Advance action.

Force

A weapon with the 'Force' special rule counts as having the 'Sniper' special rule against units with the 'Daemonic' or 'Psyker' special rules. Weapons with 'Force' may only be used if the unit is unbroken.

New Rules - Psyker Special Rules

Psyker - Prosperine Arcana

If there is at least one unit with the 'Psyker - Prosperine Arcana' special rule in a formation, and it is unbroken, one unit in the formation gains the 'Invulnerable Save' special rule if it fails an armour save during an assault.

Psyker - Telepathy

If there is at least one unit with the 'Psyker - Telepathy' special rule in a formation, and it is unbroken, it counts as having one less BM during assaults. A formation cannot have less than zero BMs.

Psyker - Thaumaturgy

If there is at least one unit with the 'Psyker - Thaumaturgy' special rule in a formation, and it is unbroken, the formation may re-roll one failed armour, Invulnerable Save, or Regeneration roll during assaults. Remember that any dice can only be re-rolled once.

Psyker - Telekinesis

If there is at least one unit with the 'Psyker - Telekinesis' special rule in a formation, and it is unbroken, one enemy unit is prevented from counter charging during assaults the psyker is directly involved in.

Psyker - Pyromancy

If there is at least one unit with the 'Psyker - Pyromancy' special rule in a formation, and it is unbroken, the formation may re-roll one failed FF attack when directly involved in an assault.

Psyker - Divination

If there is at least one unit with the 'Psyker - Divination' special rule in a formation, it does not suffer a -1 to the initiative when attempting to retain the initiative. May not be used if there are any unbroken enemy units with the 'Psyker' or 'Daemonic' special rules within 30 cm of the unit.

Psyker - Biomancy

If there is at least one unit with the 'Psyker - Biomancy' special rule in a formation, and it is unbroken, the formation may re-roll one failed CC attack during assaults.

Psyker - Anathemata

If there is at least one unit with the 'Psyker - Anathemata' special rule in a formation, and it is unbroken, one enemy formation that loses an assault against it count as having lost roll-off by an additional D3 for determining the number of hackdowns suffered.

Psyker - Aetherbane

If there is at least one unit with the 'Psyker - Aetherbane' special rule in a formation, and it is unbroken, enemy teleporting units suffer teleport mishap on 1 and 2 within 30 cm instead of the usual 15 cm.

Psyker - Diabolism

If there is at least one unit with the 'Psyker - Diabolism' special rule in a formation, and it is unbroken, the formation counts as having D3 more units when determining outnumber during an assault.

Psyker - Storm's Fury

If there is at least one unit with the 'Psyker - Storm's Fury' special rule in a formation, and it is unbroken, one unit in the formation may move an additional 5 cm when assaulting or counter charging.

Psyker - Winds of Fenris

If there is at least one unit with the 'Psyker - Winds of Fenris' special rule in a formation, and it is unbroken, one additional unit in the formation counts as being in Cover when targeted by shooting.

EA:Ragnar√∂k - Heresy rule interpretations & house-rules

Say it and its true

Effectively lots of things may move on a table. If a player is able to put a unit 6cm away from another unit, or 10cm into terrain, then saying they are doing so makes it so. This means if later a piece is budged say to cause intermingle, it won't be treated as such. - If you think it can't be done, advise your opponent when they 'say it'.

Contacting more than one enemy unit during an engage

A gray zone in the rules is under what conditions a unit can touch more than one enemy unit when it engages. While units must move towards enemy units once they enter their zone of control is clear, but less clear is how creative with angling your models in the phase you can be. Our suggestion is to be liberal in interpreting this, and that engaging or counter charging units can always contact up to two enemy units if they have enough speed to do so, and there is enough physical space to fit.

When is a formation 'in a position to support'?

After an Engage has been resolved, formations on the losing side recieves a Blast Marker, but it is not entirely clear exactly at which point during the Engage that this is determined. Our suggestion is that formations with units that has rolled FF dice (or could have been allowed to roll FF dice if they had an FF value or had not been broken), are considered to have been in a position to support. This resolves the situation in which units from the enemy formation were within 15cm from a formation at the start of the Engage, but were eliminated before the supporting formation had a chance to support.

Resolving hits from Supporting attacks

In order to determine the direction and order of hits from supporting fire, such hits are resolved one supporting formation at a time, in any order that the controlling player wishes.

Preserve allocation order between normal and macro hits

Macro and standard hits are resolved in separate phases of shooting and assaults. We suggest you preserve the allocation order between the two phases. I.e. Imperial Guard with a Gorgon assault and receive 2 normal hits and 1 TK. The Gorgon and guardsmen are equally close to the enemy. If the Imperial Guard player choose to take the normal hits into the Gorgon, and it doesnt die, he must also take the TK into the Gorgon. Likewise if he take the regular hits into the guardsmen and they dont die the TK goes into the guardsmen. If the Guardsmen die from the normal hits the TK goes into the nearest surviving unit to the enemy.

Formations mixing BP and standard weapons

As far as we understand there are no rules defining how things work when a formation fires both BP and standard weapons. We suggest the following house-rule: A formation can chose to fire it's BP weapons before or after any non-BP weapons has been fired. This includes Macro and Titan Killer BP weapons (an exception to the macro-always-goes-last rule). Resolve the BP shooting as a separate phase i.e. allocate and resolve hits from the BP weapon before moving on to ordinary weapons. Hits from ordinary weapons should be allocated front-to-back, as usual. This means that units that were hit by the BP weapon and survived can be allocated a second hit from standard shooting.

Maximize the effect of special rules when shooting

There are cases where some of the weapons fired has special rules while others don't. It could be Tau Railgun Hammerheads (where some of the AT fire has Lance) or a Baneblade where one of the weapons has Ignore Cover . Hits are (as always) allocated from the front to the back but if it matters how the hits with special rules are allocated, if should be done to favor of the shooting formation. E.g. A Baneblade fires at orks, where three orks are in the open while the rest is in a house (4+ cover save). The Baneblade fires all its weapons into cover (to be able to hit all the Orks). It scores four AP hits without special rules and one with the ignore cover special rule. In this case the ignore cover hit should not be allocated to any of the three Orks hit in the open but to one of the two Orks in the house to maximize the effect of ignore cover. Another example would be the Hammerheads shooting at a Chaos Armoured Company of two Predators and two Land Raiders. The Hammerheads score one standard hit and two with lance. The Predators are in front of the Chaos formation and will take the two first hits. The remaining hit, allocated to the Land Raider, should be one of the two hits with lance.

Disembarking & embarking ground transports

When disembarking from a transport the disembarking unit should be placed within 5cm of the transport. Only a part of the model needs to be within 5cm of the transport vehicle, not the whole model. When embarking on a ground transport the transport needs to touch the unit it is picking up.

Disembarking & embarking air transports

When disembarking from an air transport the disembarking unit should be placed within 5cm (or 15cm if the unit has jump packs) of the transport. Only a part of the model needs to be within 5cm (or 15cm) of the transport vehicle, not the whole model. When an air transport lands to pick up a formation, all units within (any part of the model) 5cm of the transport can be picked up. The air transport can land on (barge) the units it is picking up. This way you don't have to prepare a free "landing zone" inside a large formation in order to get all units in the formation in range to be picked up. A formation may also move on to a landed air transport, in which case all units needs to touch the landed air transport in order to embark (no 5cm "pickup zone" in this case)

Barging with transport aircraft

The rules does not explicitly allow or disallow transport aircraft from barging, but does state that barging is made as part of movement. But as aircraft landing or taking off is not considered movement for Overwatch purposes, it seems most consistent to not allow transport aircraft to barge.

Broken air transports

In case an air transport breaks (without dying) it can choose to stay on the board and rally like any other ground unit or to make a disengagement move. The rules for what happens if it chooses to fly off is a bit vague. We suggest the following: As soon as the unit/formation takes of in order to fly out it once again counts as an aircraft, with a number of blast markers equal to the number it took to break the unit or formation. It can (like any other aircraft) pick up additional blastmarkes on the way out, if shot by enemy flak and/or if it exits from any table edge other than its own table edge. E.g. an Ork Landa is broken by shooting on the ground and choses to fly off in the end phase. Once airborn it will have 3 blast markers (one for each DC). It is also hit by enemy flak (without being wounded) on the way out, resulting in 4 BMs when trying to activate in the next turn.

Aircraft sniping

The rules does not restrict you from placing ground attacking aircrafts inside a formation or even on top of enemy units in order to allocate hits to chosen enemy characters or units. This feels a bit gamy, and we suggest the following house-rule: Aircrafts on a ground attack mission may not be placed within 5cm of an enemy unit in the formation it is targeting. The restriction is always 5cm even for units with the Scout special rule.

Always roll for critical hits

Players must always roll for critical hits in any situation where the critical hit may affect units or formations around the war engine, even if the War Engine was reduced to 0 DC before crits have been rolled for.

Terrain height, skimmers & hills

Terrain and War Engines are "infinitely high" to ground units with regards to line of sight. No ground unit can see directly through a piece of terrain ‚Äì unless they are in it. Popped up skimmers can see over terrain, but can only see units that are further away from the edge of the terrain piece than they are. Same rule as for popped up skimmers also apply for units on hills. Aircrafts can always see over all terrain. In addition, see the 'Terrain' section on this website.

Skimmers, terrain and movement

Skimmers only checks for dangerous and difficult terrain at the start and at the end of their total movement, unless otherwise stated by the player. Skimmers that ignore intervening terrain can obviously not claim cover from such terrain, and counts as being popped up for Overwatch purposes.
